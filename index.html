The Problem

The core rewards problems

Two primary gaps in the rewards industry.

01
All these rewards, and I don't know how to use them.

Unsavvy rewards holders sit on balances they don't know how to use. The value is locked up behind complexity, transfer charts, and expiring miles.

Opportunity hypothesis

An automated, Travel-AI redemption layer that finds and books the highest-value award travel for people sitting on idle points. The bet: real demand, and willingness to pay to finally extract full value.

02
With no credit card, you don't get meaningful rewards.

Credit-averse consumers are increasingly debit-first, so they never get the chance to accumulate meaningful points in the first place.

Opportunity hypothesis

A debit card that lets non-credit consumers earn transferable points. We monetize the deposit float and the creation of the points currency itself: revenue from points creation, not points transfer.

The Thesis

Key findings from expert conversations

01
The moat is real

PJ Tudisco (M&T) and Daryl Bible, the CFO who watched the Delta debit card die at Truist, both confirm big banks are structurally locked out by Durbin and network politics. It's a regulatory moat, not a product gap.

 02
The stack already exists

Lithic (processor), a Durbin-exempt bank, Currency Alliance (ledger + transfers), Duffel (search/booking). We build the experience; everything else is API.

 03
The customer is validated

"New to Rewards": 49M credit-averse adults, 85M+ budget-airline flyers. Currency Alliance's CEO confirmed these are high-LTV users airlines and hotels actively want to acquire.

 04
It's already tangible

Marketing demo and platform pilot are both live and demoable today.

"Research continues to show a large number of travelers, particularly younger consumers such as Gen Z, prefer debit cards as their primary method of payment. Unfortunately, that means many have been left on the sidelines when it comes to earning free hotel nights and other travel rewards."

The Plan

Two-phase GTM: validation gates the launch spend

Phase 1
Validation

Sandbox + fake-door landing page + referral waitlist + tight ad test. Secure the bank's conditional approval to launch. Prove demand before any launch capital moves. The < $50K question.

Phase 2
Lightning Strike

Only after the gate clears and bank + capital are in place: a single coordinated push to ~25K users from the waitlist. Not a gradual rollout. We release the built-up pressure all at once.
